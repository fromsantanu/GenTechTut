# Chapter 23: Why Models Behave Differently

---

# 📘 Introduction

In previous chapters, we compared:

* GANs
* Diffusion models
* Their architectures
* Their optimization behavior

Now we ask a deeper question:

> Why do these models behave so differently during learning?

For example:

| Model     | Behavior       |
| --------- | -------------- |
| GAN       | Often unstable |
| Diffusion | Usually stable |

Both use:

* Neural networks
* Backpropagation
* Gradient descent

So why do their learning behaviors differ so much?

The answer lies in:

* Loss design
* Optimization dynamics
* Gradient flow
* Learning objectives

---

# 🧠 The Core Idea

Neural networks learn through:

```id="r7wz3e"
Loss
   ↓
Gradients
   ↓
Weight Updates
   ↓
Learning Behavior
```

Different models create:

* Different gradients
* Different optimization landscapes
* Different training dynamics

---

# 🎯 Why Learning Behavior Matters

Learning behavior affects:

* Stability
* Speed
* Image quality
* Diversity
* Scalability

Understanding this is one of the most important concepts in deep learning.

---

# 🎭 1. Why GANs Are Unstable

---

# 📘 GAN Learning Structure

GANs contain:

* Generator
* Discriminator

Both optimize against each other.

---

# ⚖️ GAN Objective

\min_G \max_D V(D,G)

---

# 🧠 Important Insight

GANs do not optimize toward:

* A fixed target

Instead:

* The target continuously changes

---

# 🔄 Dynamic Learning Environment

Suppose:

* Generator improves

Immediately:

* Discriminator adapts

Now:

* Generator must change strategy again

---

# 🔄 Continuous Competition

```id="q6o8dx"
Generator improves
      ↓
Discriminator adapts
      ↓
Generator changes again
      ↓
Discriminator changes again
```

This creates:

* Non-stationary optimization

---

# ⚠️ Resulting Problems

GANs commonly suffer from:

* Oscillation
* Mode collapse
* Vanishing gradients
* Training instability

---

# 📉 Oscillation

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/gxsvQNACn91SE71qME-Ads4CiwypYgLccs2Mr-D1M9EOWLYii_xhMKhhio7E5BoJnDAHRrN8UBq0Fp1PUSqun_-CtVErpl2opL48ECXwmaqNu9f9RMsldJqTKd8xNIkFgcny_eAsYALvKhMiCkG6aALcBYU56kgf27PdiofWXTEREmBhmJBc3wRcX1R-8rCU?purpose=fullsize)

The model may:

* Move back and forth
* Never fully settle

Loss curves often fluctuate heavily.

---

# ❌ Mode Collapse

The generator may discover:

* Limited outputs that fool the discriminator

Then repeatedly generate:

* Similar outputs

---

# ❌ Vanishing Gradients

If discriminator becomes too strong:

* Generator receives weak gradients

Result:

* Learning slows dramatically

---

# 🧠 Why GAN Gradients Become Problematic

GAN gradients depend on:

* Another changing neural network

This means:

* The gradient direction itself keeps changing

Optimization becomes unstable.

---

# 🌫️ 2. Why Diffusion Models Are Stable

---

# 📘 Diffusion Learning Structure

Diffusion models usually solve:

* One prediction task

---

# 🎯 Main Objective

The model learns:

> Predict the noise added to an image.

---

# 📘 Diffusion Loss

$$L = ||\epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t,t)||^2$$

---

# 🧠 Important Difference

The target:

* Does not change dynamically

The model always predicts:

* Known added noise

---

# 🎯 Result

Optimization becomes:

* Stable
* Smooth
* Predictable

---

# 📉 Stable Learning Curves

![Image](https://images.openai.com/static-rsc-4/Wf0ZNdtgB9dh-E00IQCaiDWwiDhrt0C-8_5JsK3bw0ndEhBwr7gVGL8efDFOjI9axt6fFUwgnEW7YOpAsME1s5g2GniR2cm1MQBPXF7itpATKXIpsm_SQa5lvlf1rffQHau-38EAGVUzEYJpx5JUQZLm93s8RxUOVvkMmw8ZdEipY3Va7RflVZpzYu0CSLI7?purpose=fullsize)

Loss usually decreases:

* Gradually
* Smoothly
* Predictably

---

# 🧠 Why Diffusion Gradients Are Stable

The gradients always point toward:

* Better noise prediction

The learning target remains:

* Mathematically fixed

This creates:

* Consistent gradient directions

---

# ⚖️ GAN vs Diffusion Stability

| Aspect       | GAN         | Diffusion |
| ------------ | ----------- | --------- |
| Objective    | Changing    | Fixed     |
| Optimization | Competitive | Direct    |
| Gradients    | Dynamic     | Stable    |
| Convergence  | Difficult   | Smooth    |

---

# 🧠 3. How Loss Design Affects Learning

---

# 📘 Loss Function = Learning Direction

Loss functions define:

* What the model tries to improve

The loss directly shapes:

* Gradient behavior
* Optimization dynamics

---

# ⚠️ Important Insight

Different loss designs create:

* Different learning personalities

---

# 🎭 GAN Loss Design

GAN losses create:

* Adversarial competition

Result:

* Powerful realism
* But unstable optimization

---

# 🌫️ Diffusion Loss Design

Diffusion loss creates:

* Reconstruction learning

Result:

* Stable denoising optimization

---

# 📘 Fixed vs Moving Targets

---

## GAN

Target changes continuously because:

* Opponent network changes

---

## Diffusion

Target remains fixed because:

* Noise is known mathematically

---

# 🧠 Why Fixed Targets Matter

Fixed targets create:

* Consistent gradients
* Predictable updates
* Stable convergence

---

# 🧠 Why Moving Targets Cause Problems

Moving targets create:

* Conflicting gradients
* Oscillation
* Instability

---

# ⚙️ 4. Role of Gradient Flow

---

# 📘 What Are Gradients?

Gradients tell the model:

> “Which direction reduces loss?”

---

# 🧠 Gradient Flow

Gradient flow refers to:

* How learning signals move through the network

Good gradient flow:

* Stable learning

Poor gradient flow:

* Weak or unstable learning

# ⚠️ GAN Gradient Problems

GANs may experience:

* Sudden gradient changes
* Weak gradients
* Conflicting optimization signals

---

# ❌ Vanishing Gradients

If discriminator becomes too strong:

```id="s9q0z5"
Generator Gradient → Near Zero
```

Generator stops learning effectively.

---

# ❌ Exploding Gradients

Large unstable updates may occur.

Result:

* Numerical instability
* Divergence

---

# 🌫️ Diffusion Gradient Flow

Diffusion models usually produce:

* Smooth gradients
* Consistent updates

---

# 📌 Why?

Because:

* Noise prediction creates stable optimization surfaces

---

# 🧠 Small Prediction Corrections

Diffusion models learn:

* Small denoising improvements

This creates:

* Gradual refinement
* Stable updates

---

# 🧩 Real-Life Analogy

---

## 🎭 GAN

Like:

* Two people constantly changing rules during a game

Hard to stabilize.

---

## 🌫️ Diffusion

Like:

* Solving a fixed puzzle step-by-step

Much easier to optimize.

# 🧠 Architecture Also Affects Behavior

Different architectures influence:

* Gradient flow
* Optimization stability

---

# 📘 CNNs

Usually:

* Stable gradients
* Strong local feature learning

---

# 📘 RNNs

Can suffer:

* Vanishing gradients over time

---

# 📘 Transformers

Improve:

* Long-range gradient flow
* Parallel learning

---

# 📘 Diffusion U-Nets

Improve:

* Multi-scale denoising
* Stable feature propagation

---

# 🧠 Why Optimization Is Central to AI

Modern AI progress depends heavily on:

* Better optimization
* Better gradient behavior
* Better loss design

Not just:

* Bigger models

# 🧭 Big Picture Understanding

Different AI models behave differently because:

> Their loss functions create different gradient behaviors and optimization landscapes.

GANs:

* Learn through competition

Diffusion:

* Learn through reconstruction

The entire learning behavior emerges from:

* How gradients are generated and propagated.

---

# 🎯 Key Insight

The biggest reason diffusion models are stable is:

> Their gradients consistently point toward a fixed denoising objective.

The biggest reason GANs are unstable is:

> Their gradients depend on another continuously changing neural network.
